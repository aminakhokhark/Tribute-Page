Marie Curie

The first women to win a Nobel Prize

Here is a time line of Marie Curie's life:

  • 1867 - Born as Maria Salomea Sklodowska in Warsaw, Poland.
  • 1891 - Marie moved to Paris, France and continued her education at University of Paris.
  • 1893 - Marie was awarded dergree in physics and second degree in 1894.
  • 1895 - Marie at the time was studying magnetic properties. She recived Ph.D for her research for magnetism.
  • 1895 - Marie got married to Pierre Curie,who helped her at the very begginning with lab space and convinced her to do Ph.D.
  • 1896 - After Uranium was dicovered by Henri Becquerel, Marie started stadying two uranium minerals (pitchblende and torbernite) with electrometer, invetnted by her husband.Her electrometer showed that pitchblen was foure times as active as uranium it self and torbernite two times, which made her belive that these two minerals must contain quatities of another substance far more active than uranium.
  • 1898 - Together with her husband, Pierre, Marie published joint paper announcing existence of an element they named "polonium" in honour of Marie's native Poland.In December the same year Marie annouced existance of second element, which they names "radium".
  • 1898-1902 - The Curies published, jointly or separately, a total of 32 scientific papers, including one that announced that, when exposed to radium, diseased, tumour-forming cells were destroyed faster than healthy cells.
  • 1903 - Marie, together with her husband Pierre and Henri Becquerel, was awarded by Royal Swedish Academy of Science the Nobel Prize in Psysics, which made her the first women in history to recive a Nobel Prize.
  • 1911 - Marie recived her second Nobel Prize, this time in Chemistry.

Marie Curie was an extraordinary women, who's life wasn't easy, from poverty while young, to sexism at work, she remained focused on her work no matter what. She had two dauthers as well but never compromised on her academic life.
